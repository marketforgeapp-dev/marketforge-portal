# Internal Execution Guide

MARKETFORGE INTERNAL OPERATIONAL GUIDE  
ACTION / CAMPAIGN EXECUTION GUIDE  
VERSION: FOUNDER-LED EXECUTION PHASE

PURPOSE

This guide explains exactly how MarketForge team members should execute customer campaigns and actions across all supported channels.

This is written for someone with:  
\- no prior marketing agency experience  
\- no prior ad platform experience  
\- no prior SEO / AEO publishing experience

If you follow this guide step by step, you should be able to:  
1\. understand what to launch  
2\. know where to launch it  
3\. know what assets to use  
4\. know how to publish it correctly  
5\. know how to document what was done  
6\. know how to avoid common mistakes

This is an INTERNAL guide for MarketForge operators.  
This is not customer-facing.

\=====================================================================  
SECTION 1 — CORE OPERATING PRINCIPLES  
\=====================================================================

1\. MARKETFORGE IS THE OPERATOR  
We are not just generating ideas.  
We are responsible for executing approved actions correctly.

2\. ONLY EXECUTE APPROVED PLATFORMS  
If a platform/asset is not approved in the Action Detail page, do not launch it.  
Approved assets are the source of truth.

3\. EXECUTION MUST MATCH THE ACTION  
Do not improvise a different service, offer, audience, or CTA.  
Use the approved assets and approved action framing.

4\. TRUST \> SPEED  
A correct launch tomorrow is better than a sloppy launch today.

5\. DOCUMENT EVERYTHING  
For every launch, note:  
\- date launched  
\- who launched it  
\- platforms launched  
\- links or screenshots proving launch  
\- any issues or deviations

6\. NEVER GUESS WITH CUSTOMER BRAND  
If you are unsure about:  
\- logo use  
\- wording  
\- contact info  
\- offer details  
\- service area  
\- imagery  
stop and verify before launching.

\=====================================================================  
SECTION 2 — BEFORE YOU LAUNCH ANY ACTION  
\=====================================================================

Every campaign launch starts with the same pre-launch checklist.

PRE-LAUNCH CHECKLIST

1\. Open the Action Detail page  
2\. Confirm campaign status is APPROVED  
3\. Go to Launch Asset Review  
4\. Confirm every platform has been either:  
   \- APPROVED  
   \- REMOVED  
5\. Confirm export pack exists  
6\. Confirm target service is correct  
7\. Confirm offer is correct  
8\. Confirm CTA is correct  
9\. Confirm business name, phone, website, and logo are correct  
10\. Confirm imagery aligns with the actual service  
11\. Confirm no placeholder text exists  
12\. Confirm no conflicting offers exist across channels

DO NOT LAUNCH if:  
\- assets still need review  
\- wrong service imagery is used  
\- CTA is unclear  
\- offer is wrong  
\- contact info is wrong  
\- platform is not approved

SECTION 5 — GOOGLE BUSINESS PROFILE EXECUTION  
\=====================================================================

WHEN TO USE  
Use when Google Business Profile is approved in the action.

WHAT YOU NEED  
\- approved GBP post copy  
\- approved image  
\- customer Google Business Profile access  
\- destination URL or booking URL  
\- CTA from approved asset

WHERE TO EXECUTE  
Google Business Profile dashboard / business profile manager

STEP-BY-STEP

1\. Log into the customer’s Google Business Profile account  
2\. Open the correct business location  
3\. Go to the section for Posts / Updates  
4\. Select the standard post/update format unless the action specifically requires an offer or event format  
5\. Upload the approved image  
6\. Paste the approved GBP title/headline if applicable  
7\. Paste the approved GBP body copy  
8\. Add the CTA button that best matches the approved CTA  
   Common CTA mapping:  
   \- Book now → Book  
   \- Call now → Call  
   \- Learn more → Learn more  
9\. Add the correct destination URL  
10\. Review carefully before publishing

CHECK BEFORE PUBLISHING  
\- image matches service  
\- copy is readable and not cut off awkwardly  
\- phone/URL is correct  
\- offer matches approved action  
\- service area and service type are correct

AFTER PUBLISHING  
\- take screenshot of live post  
\- copy live URL if available  
\- log date/time in execution log

COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID  
\- publishing wrong service image  
\- using homepage instead of correct destination page  
\- posting outdated offer  
\- using generic copy instead of approved copy

\=====================================================================  
SECTION 6 — FACEBOOK EXECUTION  
\=====================================================================

WHEN TO USE  
Use when Meta asset is approved.  
Current policy: one approved Meta asset is used for both Facebook and Instagram.

WHAT YOU NEED  
\- approved Meta headline  
\- approved Meta primary text  
\- approved CTA  
\- approved image  
\- destination URL  
\- access to customer Facebook page and/or ad account

DECIDE WHETHER THIS IS:  
A. organic page post  
B. boosted post  
C. ad in Meta Ads Manager

For current founder-led execution, default to:  
\- organic/boosted if appropriate for the customer  
\- use Ads Manager only if the account is set up and support process is clear

FACEBOOK ORGANIC POST — STEP-BY-STEP

1\. Log into the customer Facebook Page  
2\. Start a new post  
3\. Upload approved image  
4\. Paste approved primary text  
5\. If appropriate, include destination URL  
6\. Review formatting  
7\. Publish or schedule

FACEBOOK BOOSTED POST — STEP-BY-STEP

1\. Publish the organic post first  
2\. Click Boost Post  
3\. Choose business objective closest to approved action  
4\. Set service area / local geography  
5\. Set budget and duration per internal account plan  
6\. Confirm CTA button  
7\. Confirm landing URL  
8\. Launch boost  
9\. Screenshot targeting/budget before publishing if possible

FACEBOOK ADS MANAGER — STEP-BY-STEP

1\. Open customer ad account  
2\. Create campaign using approved objective  
3\. Create ad set:  
   \- local targeting  
   \- correct audience  
   \- budget and duration  
4\. Create ad:  
   \- approved image  
   \- approved headline  
   \- approved primary text  
   \- approved CTA  
   \- correct URL  
5\. Review all placements  
6\. Publish  
7\. Document campaign ID and screenshots

CHECK BEFORE PUBLISHING  
\- headline and body match approved content  
\- image is correct  
\- local targeting is correct  
\- URL is correct  
\- budget is intentional and documented

COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID  
\- wrong business page  
\- wrong local targeting  
\- inconsistent offer vs approved action  
\- using a stock image that mismatches service

SECTION 7 — INSTAGRAM EXECUTION  
\=====================================================================

WHEN TO USE  
Use when Meta asset is approved.  
Current policy: same Meta asset is used for Instagram.

WHAT YOU NEED  
\- approved image  
\- approved Meta primary text adapted as caption  
\- approved CTA  
\- correct profile access  
\- destination link method

IMPORTANT  
Instagram links are limited.  
If link-in-bio is required:  
\- update only if approved and documented  
\- do not overwrite important live links without approval

STEP-BY-STEP

1\. Log into the customer Instagram account  
2\. Create a new post  
3\. Upload approved square image  
4\. Paste approved caption/copy  
5\. If needed, adapt line breaks so it is readable  
6\. Include clear CTA:  
   \- call today  
   \- visit website  
   \- click link in bio  
7\. Publish or schedule  
8\. Document with screenshot

CHECK BEFORE PUBLISHING  
\- image is square and looks clean  
\- caption is readable  
\- CTA makes sense for Instagram  
\- link method is clear

COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID  
\- forgetting that Instagram link behavior is limited  
\- using a wide image that crops badly  
\- posting copy written for another platform without cleanup

\=====================================================================  
SECTION 8 — GOOGLE ADS EXECUTION  
\=====================================================================

WHEN TO USE  
Use when Google Ads asset is approved.

WHAT YOU NEED  
\- approved headlines  
\- approved descriptions  
\- correct landing page  
\- correct service geography  
\- customer Google Ads access  
\- budget guidance

VERY IMPORTANT  
Do not launch Google Ads casually.  
If account structure is messy, document it before changes.

STEP-BY-STEP

1\. Log into the customer Google Ads account  
2\. Identify whether to:  
   \- create new campaign  
   \- create new ad group within an existing relevant campaign  
3\. Choose campaign type appropriate to current execution plan  
   Usually Search for this phase  
4\. Set geography:  
   \- customer city/service area  
   \- do not overbroaden without reason  
5\. Set budget  
6\. Add keyword targeting aligned to approved service  
7\. Create ad using approved headlines/descriptions  
8\. Use correct final URL  
9\. Add call extensions / location extensions if already configured  
10\. Review policy compliance  
11\. Publish  
12\. Document campaign/ad group/ad IDs

CHECK BEFORE PUBLISHING  
\- service intent matches approved action  
\- geography is local and correct  
\- landing page is correct  
\- no headline exceeds Google limits  
\- phone/location extensions are not broken

COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID  
\- sending traffic to homepage when service page exists  
\- broad targeting outside service area  
\- using wrong service language  
\- launching on exact wrong match/service mismatch

SECTION 11 — BLOG EXECUTION  
\=====================================================================

WHEN TO USE  
Use when Blog asset is approved.

WHAT YOU NEED  
\- approved blog title  
\- excerpt  
\- intro  
\- body sections  
\- CTA  
\- website CMS access  
\- correct category/tag if used  
\- feature image if applicable

STEP-BY-STEP

1\. Log into the customer website CMS  
2\. Create a new blog post  
3\. Paste the approved title  
4\. Paste excerpt if CMS supports excerpt/meta description  
5\. Paste intro and section body content  
6\. Format headings correctly:  
   \- Title \= H1  
   \- main sections \= H2  
   \- subpoints only if needed  
7\. Add internal links where appropriate:  
   \- service page  
   \- contact page  
   \- booking page  
8\. Add CTA section at the end  
9\. Add approved feature image if applicable  
10\. Set URL slug cleanly  
11\. Preview on desktop/mobile  
12\. Publish  
13\. Save live URL and screenshot

CHECK BEFORE PUBLISHING  
\- no broken formatting  
\- heading structure is clean  
\- service/location references are correct  
\- CTA is present  
\- no AI artifact text remains

COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID  
\- pasting unformatted blocks  
\- no heading structure  
\- broken internal links  
\- publishing with “draft” placeholder content

\=====================================================================  
SECTION 13 — AEO FAQ EXECUTION  
\=====================================================================

WHEN TO USE  
Use when AEO FAQ asset is approved.

PURPOSE  
This is meant to improve answer-engine visibility and direct-answer readiness.

WHERE TO PLACE  
Usually:  
\- FAQ page  
\- relevant service page  
\- relevant local service section  
\- schema-enabled FAQ block if site supports it

STEP-BY-STEP

1\. Identify the best page for the FAQ content  
2\. Open page editor in website CMS  
3\. Add approved FAQ questions and answers  
4\. Format clearly:  
   \- question  
   \- answer directly below  
5\. If schema plugin/support exists, use it correctly  
6\. Avoid duplicate FAQ blocks across too many pages unless intentional  
7\. Publish  
8\. Save live URL and screenshot

CHECK BEFORE PUBLISHING  
\- questions are clear  
\- answers are direct and useful  
\- content matches actual services offered  
\- no false claims  
\- no strange formatting

COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID  
\- posting generic FAQ content unrelated to the actual customer  
\- adding FAQ to irrelevant pages  
\- failing to publish changes live

\=====================================================================  
SECTION 14 — ANSWER SNIPPET EXECUTION  
\=====================================================================

WHEN TO USE  
Use when Answer Snippet content is approved.

PURPOSE  
This content should help the site answer common questions directly and clearly.

STEP-BY-STEP

1\. Identify the best destination page  
2\. Insert approved answer content near the relevant section  
3\. Use short, direct explanatory formatting  
4\. If possible, place near relevant heading/question  
5\. Publish  
6\. Save proof of launch

CHECK BEFORE PUBLISHING  
\- answer is direct  
\- page topic matches  
\- no duplicate/conflicting answers nearby

COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID  
\- burying answer in irrelevant content  
\- leaving answer too vague  
\- publishing where customer cannot support the claim operationally

\=====================================================================  
SECTION 15 — WEBSITE CMS OPERATIONS RULES  
\=====================================================================

Whenever editing a customer website:

ALWAYS  
\- back up existing content first  
\- note page URL before editing  
\- preview before publishing  
\- screenshot before/after if change is significant

NEVER  
\- delete major existing content without reason  
\- change nav/site structure casually  
\- overwrite customer branding  
\- publish half-finished edits

IF YOU DO NOT HAVE CMS ACCESS  
\- document exact requested changes  
\- prepare copy in clean format  
\- hand off for implementation  
\- log that execution is pending access

\=====================================================================  
SECTION 16 — IMAGE HANDLING RULES  
\=====================================================================

CURRENT OPERATING MODEL  
Images are currently:  
\- stock images  
\- customer-provided images  
\- service-aligned imagery selected manually

RULES  
1\. Image must match service  
2\. Do not use plumbing imagery for HVAC, etc.  
3\. Do not use emergency imagery for routine services unless appropriate  
4\. Do not use logo when service image is required unless specifically approved  
5\. If image quality is poor, stop and escalate

IMAGE QA CHECKLIST  
\- correct service  
\- readable crop on target platform  
\- no distortion  
\- no unrelated tools/equipment shown  
\- no visible watermark  
\- brand-safe

SECTION 17 — HOW TO USE THE EXPORT PACK  
\=====================================================================

The export pack is the operator’s launch kit.

ALWAYS REVIEW:  
\- 00-START-HERE  
\- campaign brief  
\- approved platform folders  
\- manifest  
\- checklist

HOW TO USE IT  
1\. Open 00-START-HERE  
2\. Read the action summary  
3\. Read what platforms are active vs inactive  
4\. Open platform folder  
5\. Copy approved content exactly  
6\. Launch on that platform  
7\. document proof  
8\. move to next approved platform

IF A FOLDER IS EMPTY OR SAYS NO APPROVED ASSET  
That means:  
\- do not launch that platform  
\- document as not included in this action

\=====================================================================  
SECTION 18 — POST-LAUNCH QA  
\=====================================================================

After every campaign is launched, do a post-launch QA pass.

POST-LAUNCH QA CHECKLIST  
\- live screenshot captured  
\- URL or live proof stored  
\- CTA works  
\- destination page works  
\- customer branding is correct  
\- copy is readable on live platform  
\- launch logged internally  
\- campaign status updated in MarketForge

\=====================================================================  
SECTION 19 — HOW TO UPDATE MARKETFORGE AFTER LAUNCH  
\=====================================================================

After launch:

1\. Go to Execution page  
2\. Confirm launch details  
3\. Mark action as launched  
4\. Confirm status changed  
5\. Add any notes internally  
6\. If something could not be launched, note exactly why

If launch is partial:  
\- do not pretend full launch occurred  
\- log which channels launched and which did not

\=====================================================================  
SECTION 20 — FAILURE / ESCALATION RULES  
\=====================================================================

ESCALATE IF:  
\- account access is missing  
\- wrong business profile/page/account is connected  
\- destination page is broken  
\- imagery is wrong or unavailable  
\- customer legal/compliance concern appears  
\- approved content contains factual problem  
\- platform rejects content  
\- site CMS access is broken  
\- ad account billing is broken

WHEN ESCALATING, DOCUMENT:  
\- what happened  
\- where it happened  
\- screenshot/proof  
\- what is blocked  
\- what is needed to proceed

\=====================================================================  
SECTION 21 — INTERNAL QUALITY STANDARD  
\=====================================================================

A launch is considered GOOD only if:

1\. It used approved assets  
2\. It matched the approved service/action  
3\. It launched on the correct platforms  
4\. It was documented  
5\. A non-expert customer could look at it and believe:  
   “Yes, this is professional, coherent, and ready to help my business.”

That is the standard.
